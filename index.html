    Interests

    Applied Metrics - Dynamic Systems

    I have been fascinated by transmission mechanisms since an early age and began my academic career as a Mechanical Engineer studying rigid physical systems at the Cooper Union for Advancement of Science and Art (four-year full-tuition scholar). Unlike the steel beams and automobile chassis of my undergraduate training, the transmissions between various features of our economy are dynamic and challenging to properly identify. In the past few years I discovered a personal passion in extracting these dynamic empirical research.

    Working as a Research Analyst in the Macroeconomic and Monetary Studies function of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York (FRBNY) Research Group opened my eyes to the wider world of academic research. Having seen the dedication to publishing research, and having felt the immense satisfaction so derived, I’m fully confident in my decision to pursue academic research as an economics PhD candidate.

    I’m interested in applied dynamics factor modeling and exploring the real-time econometric techniques of Nowcasting. Original research I have done at the FRBNY has afforded me the first of many journal submission co-authorships. On the policy side of affairs at the FRBNY, I have conducted financial market modeling and policy oriented structural modeling for the expected federal funds rate lift-off. I have been tasked with considerable explicit and implicit (unconventional guidance) policy development in this historic post Great Recession and Great Easing period.

    Coincident Economic Indicator (CEI)

    I produce monthly updates of the CEI for NY, NJ, and NYC. These are single dynamic factor results from labor data. I’m independently working on a model that blends FAVAR techniques with the CEI framework, utilizing the readily available Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages (QCEW) data by industry at the 5-digit disaggregated level.

    Stock Portfolio Allocation by Sharpe's Ratio:

    The optimization routine developed implements MATLAB’s fmincon solver and handles the Sharpe quadratic programming problem (QP) of portfolio volatility to return above the risk-free rate. A time evolution of stock allocation results.

    Forecasting Financial Conditions

    Currently, co-authoring a Factor-Augmented Vector Autoregressive (FAVAR) paper with Drs. Marc Giannoni and Ernst Schaumburg. We implement a unique algorithm that restricts estimated macroeconomic and financial block factors to be orthogonal to each other and a FFR policy instrument. I derived a sub-routine in our convergence algorithm to simultaneously check the null space of block factor loadings.
